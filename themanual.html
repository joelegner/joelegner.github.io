Be or Do

Be or do. Just be sometimes. Rest. Give yourself permission to rest. Give yourself permission to play. Let your mind wander.

And when it is time to be active, Do the right thing.

Live Life.

Do the right thing

Do the right thing according to your values. Act with love. Act with integrity. Treat others the way they want to be treated. Develop as a musician. Develop as a computer programmer. Read and learn new things. Set goals and work toward them. Exercise regularly.

Be or Do. Live Life.

Act with love

Love yourself and your wife, children, family, friends, coworkers, and fellow members of society. Be kind to yourself. Be kind to other people. Be kind to animals. Treat all beings with compassion, including you. Treat yourself the way you want to be treated and Treat others the way they want to be treated. Look upon others with eyes of kindness.

Do the right thing. Live Life.

Act with integrity

Do not lie, unless you have to lie for safety’s sake. Be as truthful, open, and honest as you can. Put forth effort to keep your commitments. Apologize when you are in the wrong.

Say to others, “I am no better than you, and you are no better than me. People are incomparable.”

Do the right thing. Live Life.

Treat yourself the way you want to be treated

Be kind to yourself. Forgive yourself. Do not overwork yourself. Stay loose. Do not fight your own nature. Understand it and accept it. Forgive yourself for shortcomings, failures, let-downs. Reminder yourself you are just a fucked-up human being. Shrug and say to yourself or others, “I’m just a fucked up human being like everyone else.”

Sit down and observe your mind, how it works, what thoughts it generates. Notice patterns in the thoughts. Notice thoughts come from nowwhere and remind yourself, “Thoughts think themselves.” Do not fight your thoughts. Accept them and Do the right thing instead.

Act with love. Live Life.

Treat others the way they want to be treated

Get to know people. Find out how they want to be treated. Treat them the way they want to be treated. Treat strangers the way you want to be treated.

Do the right thing. Act with love. Live Life.

Grow

Always grow. Develop as a musician. Develop as a computer programmer, Read and learn new things. Improve The Manual.

Live Life.

Develop as a musician

Study and practice piano to play dueling pianos. Learn chords. Work on muscle memory with repetition. Get some left hand techniques going. Play along with recordings. Play piano only with a metronome. Sing and play together like you would at a show.

Study and practice drums. Work on your stick control. Work on playing the entire set. Play together with Julie. Play dueling pianos gigs, live.

Practice key drums. Work on key drums to play with Julie in November. Think about the skills you need. Work out the best fingering. Be consistent. Play together with Julie.

Study and practice guitar. Play Ladies Man songs at home. Sing with them. Keep adding new songs. Keep things fresh and fun. Play Ladies Man songs at open mic nights. Book and play Ladies Man gigs.

Experiement with music. Try playing bass lines with the Midi keyboard.

Do the right thing. Grow. Live Life.

Do the right thing. Grow. Live Life.

Set goals and work toward them

Have a few short-term, medium-term, and long-term goals in life. Make a plan for each one. Go out and execute your plan. Enjoy when you achieve a goal. Learn from your mistakes. Goals bring long-term satisfaction.

Pick goals that will help you exemplify your core values. Work toward them. Go on adventures. Enrich your life by stimulating yourself with new creative things to do.

“My fundamental goal is richness of life.” – Joe Legner

Put your goals in ONE PLACE. Write goals in Apple Reminders. Do not include goals in The Manual. Refer yourself to Apple Reminders

Use the year and quarter as your goals. Review those lists regularly, if not daily. This keeps the goals fresh in your mind. Check off those you complete. Enjoy that feeling! Celebrate!

“One of the most dangerous forms of human error is forgetting what one is trying to achieve.” – Paul Nietze1

Celebrate 85% to 95% goal achievement.

When you set a goal, think immediately about the steps needed to achieve it. Make a plan. A plan is a series of events you think will result in the desired outcome.

“A goal without steps is merely a wish.”

Caution: Do not worry too much about goals. Life is meant to be lived. There is no “winning” at the end of things. You can win along the way. But the end is just an end – an end of winning and an end of losing.

“Life’s greatest prize is the game itself.”

Aspire to reach your goals – do not expect to achieve them. Remember there are factors beyond your control that contribute to each outcome. Cultivate a feeling of aspirtation. Be hopeful for success but gracious in failure.

“If you don’t achieve your goals, it’s okay. If you do achieve your goals, it’s better.”

Follow these steps for goal-seeking behavior:

  1. Set goals. State them clearly. Make each one something that can be independently verified.
  2. Prioritize them.
  3. For the top goal, write down the steps you will need to do to achieve it. Remember, “A goal without steps is merely a wish.”
  4. Once you complete a goal, do not go to the next one on the priority list. Instead, re-prioritize the list.

Use the ABC method of prioritizing goals:

A = most important and most urgent
B = less important and less urgent
C = least important and least urgent; hold in reserve”

Do the right thing. Live Life.

Exercise regularly

Go for a walk. Do it now if you are up for it. At any rate, walk daily if you can. Work out with weights 2 or 3 times per week. Build muscle. Build strength and endurance. Do some stretching. Keep your body strong and limber.

Do the right thing. Live Life.

Study and practice piano

Find some piano instruction in the form of videos and books. Study them. Start with the basics. Treat piano like a percussion instrument. Stay loose, always. Hold you hand in a natural position.

Remember these desidirata:

  • Fun.
  • Sufficient skill to play a gig in front of people.
  • Posture that is low energy, sustainable.
  • Technique that reduces fatigue.
  • Technique that reduces risk of injury.

Research these questions:

  1. How do I prepare to play piano?
    • Pianote interview with a chiropractor: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NGR4WaWcJA
    • Do a whole body warmup for a good 10 minutes. Get some blood flowing.
    • Stretch top of forearms by pulling hands toward you like in drum warmup. Do this for a few minutes.
    • Stretch bottom of forearms and fingers by pulling hand back like in drum warmup. Do this for a few minutes.
    • Sit up in a neutral postion, lean to one side, feel the stretch in the neck. Do not roll your neck. Come back to neutral. Do this for a few minutes.
    • Sit up in a neutral postion, lean 45 degrees forward, feel the stretch. Use your hands to gently assist. Do not roll your neck. Come back to neutral. Do this for a few minutes.
    • Sit up in a neutral postion, lean straight forward, feel the stretch. Use your hands to assist. Do not roll your neck. Come back to neutral. Do this for a few minutes.
    • Try yoga for balance, strength, and positioning.
    • Get up and move, shake it out periodically.
  2. How do I sit at the piano to avoid injury?
    • Pianote interview with a chiropractor: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NGR4WaWcJA
    • Tuck the lower back in, in a natural position. Stack up spine but be neutral. Balance on the spine.
    • Keep head up, straight above spine.
    • Do not jerk shoulders back and cause burning and tension.
    • Drop shoulders down into comfortable playing position.
