Understanding

What does it mean to understand something? Let us try on a tentative definition. Let Pi represent a proposition.

To understand new proposition Pn is to refer it to Pn − 1 where Pn − 1 is an existing proposition already understood.

This seems to lead to an infinite regress. If we understand claim Pn by reference to claim Pn − 1, we might rightly ask how we understood Pn − 1 to begin with. If we apply our definition about, now starting with Pn − 1 we find we need yet another claim Pn − 2 to understand Pn − 1.

How do we extract ourselves from the regress? We need to hit bottom. We might call this a base case. The base case must be something that does not need further regress to existing beliefs. The author holds that the parts of experience that meet this criteria are the person’s experiences of the physical world.

Would adopting these propositions as unjustified and unjustifiable axioms help?

  1. AXIOM OF EXISTENCE: Existence exists. Entities exist.
  2. AXIOM OF IDENTITY: A thing is what it is. Any entity that exists has a unique identity.
  3. AXIOM OF CONSCIOUSNESS: Consciousness is conscious of something. We are conscious of entities.
