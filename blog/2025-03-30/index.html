Why should I read this blog?

Reading the ideas on this website will help you live a better life.

That's a bold claim. In mathematics we might call such a claim a theorem. How can we prove the theorem? We can put it this way:

Theorem. Reading the ideas on this website will help you live a better life.

Proof. The theorem says this: there is a set of ideas, housed on this website, that can help a person live a better life. The proof of this is that it has helped one person already: the author. This is a form of what mathematicians call "direct proof."

Okay, the theorem and proof are a bit silly. This is not a formal proof by any means. But it gives you a sense of how I like to reason. It could be made very formal, using set theory.

I want to help people. I believe good ideas help people. So I see myself as a curator of good ideas. For now I hope you will accept the tentative definition that "good" means "conducive to achieving of goals in life." I call this role in life "The Gardner". The Gardner plants seeds and waters them.

What's with all the metaphors?

Before you go, I wanted to point out something here: metaphors. There are metaphors in this page. They are the key to understanding the site. It might be helpful to remember this.

Above I "see" myself as a curator of good ideas. I do not literally see myself that way. I see my body the way I have always seen it. Nothing has changed. What I'm describing is more like imagining a mental persona, an analog of the me here on earth. This is another metaphor.

I also claim that I am a "Garnder" that "plants seeds and waters them." This is a hint about ideas being seeds and evidence being water that helps seeds grow.

I think metaphors are critical for understanding. In fact I believe all human knowledge is layers of metaphors built on some very basic concepts like object, object permanence, weight, distance, time, containers to name a few.
